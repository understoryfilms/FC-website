About the Film

Deep in the ancient forests of Vancouver Island, on unceded Pacheedaht territory, a standoff unfolds. "Fairy Creek" documents the intense frontline battle to save the last old-growth temperate rainforests in British Columbia from logging. Follow Indigenous land defenders like Elder Bill Jones, Pacheedaht Hereditary Chief Victor Peter, Kati George-Jim, and Carole Tootill, alongside settler allies like Rainbow Eyes and Saul Arbess, as they engage in the largest act of civil disobedience in Canadian history. The film explores critical themes of Indigenous sovereignty, ecological crisis, and the urgent fight to protect these vital ecosystems for future generations.

(Directed by Travis Rummel & Felicia Muminovic, 2022, 54 min)
